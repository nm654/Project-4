The Food Chain

Every food chain for every animal starts off with the small things, and the glacier food chain is no different. Plankton is the first of the food chain, which then gets eaten by fish, which then get eaten by a variety of animals. Glaciers also affect the level of water in the sea. If the water changes too much then a massive amount of animals in the area could die from it. Seals and Polar Bears are two examples of animals that rely on glaciers, though also included are the arctic fox, whale, and caribou. Penguins also live on glaciers (or in this case, icebergs and ice floes) and rely on krill for sustenance ( a type of small organism like plankton.)

The People Surrounding Glaciers

People rely on glaciers as well as animals, some places in central Asia rely on glaciers for their water supply and South America uses the water during dry spells.. Glaciers can also be a tourist attraction while the water and ice can be popular specialty drinks. The crops in Asia are also irrigated by glacier water for dry periods. Glaciers also help power hydroelectric machines when they melt. Glacier areas are used for fishing, with examples being the Alaskan Pollock and the Trout.
